## **Common Terms & Abbreviations**

Understanding these key terms will help you navigate the pet travel process:

### **Export vs.¬†Import**

* **EXPORT** \= Pet leaves the US ‚Üí Goes to another country

* **IMPORT** \= Pet enters the US (includes US pets returning home)

* **Round-trip travel** \= Both processes apply

### [**IHC (International Health Certificate)**](https://www.aphis.usda.gov/pet-travel)

Also called an export health certificate. This is the official document required by most countries to allow your pet to enter. It includes exam findings, vaccination records, and treatments.

**To look up your destination country requirements:** Visit the [USDA APHIS Pet Travel website](https://www.aphis.usda.gov/pet-travel/us-to-another-country-export) and search for your destination country in step 2\. This is also where you‚Äôll determine if your destination is a Green Banner or Orange Banner country.

### [**CURV (Certification of US-Issued Rabies Vaccination)**](https://www.cdc.gov/importation/dogs/index.html)

Official CDC document certifying your dog has a valid US rabies vaccination. Required for easy re-entry from [high-risk rabies countries](https://www.cdc.gov/importation/dogs/high-risk-countries.html). **For dogs only.** [See Section 3B](#section-3b) for details.

### [**USDA Endorsement**](https://vehcs.aphis.usda.gov/)

Official review and stamp by the US Department of Agriculture on health certificates and CURV forms. Most countries require this. Documents are submitted electronically through VEHCS.

### **VEHCS (Veterinary Export Health Certification System)**

USDA‚Äôs online system where accredited veterinarians submit and track health certificates and CURV forms for endorsement.

### **Green Banner Country**

Countries that **accept digital USDA endorsements**. We can email you the endorsed certificate once approved. No physical shipping required.

**How to identify:** When you search for your destination country on the [USDA APHIS website](https://www.aphis.usda.gov/pet-travel/us-to-another-country-export), a **green banner** will be displayed at the top of the country page if digital endorsement is accepted. 
- **Examples:** Most South American countries 
- **Cost advantage:** No shipping fees

### **Orange Banner Country**

Countries that **require physical USDA endorsement with embossed seal**. We must overnight ship your health certificate once approved.

**How to identify:** When you search for your destination country on the [USDA APHIS website](https://www.aphis.usda.gov/pet-travel/us-to-another-country-export), an **orange banner** will be displayed at the top of the country page if physical endorsement is required. 
- **Examples:** All EU countries, United Kingdom 
- **Additional cost:** $75 overnight shipping fee

### **High-Risk Rabies Country**

Country on CDC‚Äôs rabies [high-risk list](https://www.cdc.gov/importation/dogs/high-risk-countries.html). If your destination is NOT on the list, it‚Äôs considered low-risk.

### **Primary (Initial) Rabies Vaccination**

The **first** rabies vaccination given to a dog, OR the first vaccination after any lapse in coverage (even one day when rabies vaccination expired before the next booster). Valid for **only 1 year**, even if the vaccine label says 3 years. Some countries require waiting periods after primary vaccination before travel.

## **Section 1: Common Mistakes to Avoid**

**Avoid these costly and time-consuming mistakes by planning ahead:**

### **1\. Not Having a Valid Rabies Vaccine Certificate**

**‚ùå THE \#1 DOCUMENTATION ISSUE**

**You may think you have a valid rabies vaccine certificate, but‚Ä¶**

Many certificates are missing critical information required for USDA endorsement. Even if your vet gave you a rabies certificate, it might not include everything needed for international travel.

#### *Why This Happens:*

* Many veterinarians (especially if not USDA-accredited) are unaware of specific USDA export requirements

* Certificates from other hospitals may lack required details

* Obtaining missing information can take **days or weeks** ‚Äì or may be **impossible**

* In many cases, a **repeat rabies vaccination may be needed** to ensure proper documentation

**‚Üí See [Section 4: Required Information on Rabies Certificates](#section-4) for complete details on what must be included.**

---

### **2\. Not Getting CURV Before Leaving OR Letting US Rabies Vaccination Expire (Dogs from High-Risk Countries Only)**

**‚ùå THE MOST EXPENSIVE MISTAKE**

If you‚Äôre traveling to a [**high-risk rabies country**](https://www.cdc.gov/importation/dogs/high-risk-countries.html) and planning to return to the US, you **MUST**:

1. Get a CURV (Certification of US-Issued Rabies Vaccination) **BEFORE** you leave

2. Ensure your dog‚Äôs US rabies vaccination does **NOT expire** while abroad

**If either of these fails, your dog becomes ‚Äúforeign-vaccinated‚Äù** and must follow the complex re-entry process.

#### *Without CURV or with Expired US Rabies Vaccine:*

* ‚ùå Complex, expensive re-entry process

* ‚ùå Cost: **$2,000-$7,000+**

* ‚ùå Limited entry points (only 6 airports in entire US)

* ‚ùå Mandatory facility stay (1+ nights)

* ‚ùå Requires advance facility reservations

#### *With Valid CURV and Current US Rabies Vaccine:*

* ‚úÖ Easy re-entry at any US port

* ‚úÖ No facility required

* ‚úÖ Cost: **$435** (for initial CURV)

**Solution for Extended Travel:** Get a **3-year rabies booster if your pet qualifies** before departure so your CURV stays valid longer.

**‚Üí See [Section 2, Step 3](#section-2-step-3) and [Section 3B](#section-3b) for full details**

---

### **3\. Starting Too Late OR Missing Critical Waiting Periods**

**Timeline matters\!** Some destinations require **6+ months** of preparation, and missing critical waiting periods can delay your travel.

#### *Complex Destinations (6+ months needed):*

* **Australia, New Zealand, Japan**

  * Requirements: Rabies titer testing, waiting periods, import permits, etc.

  * **‚Üí Schedule consultation immediately ‚Äì do not book travel first**

#### *European Union & UK (minimum 21 days):*

* 21-day wait required after primary rabies vaccination

* Plan accordingly if your pet needs a primary vaccine

#### *General Timeline:*

* Start **2-6+ months** before departure depending on destination

* Allow time for: research, consultations, vaccinations, waiting periods, testing, documentation

---

### **4\. Not Checking Airline Requirements**

Airlines may have requirements that are more strict than destination countries (typically a 10-day travel window requirement for a health certificate or parasite treatment needed). It‚Äôs **your responsibility** to verify and inform us of any additional requirements.

**‚Üí See [Section 5: Airline Requirements](#section-5) for complete details**

### **5\. Booking Travel Before Meeting All Requirements**

**Don‚Äôt book flights or IHC appointments until:**

* ‚òê All destination country requirements are met

* ‚òê Required waiting periods have passed

* ‚òê You‚Äôve consulted with our hospital

* ‚òê You‚Äôve confirmed airline pet policies

## **Section 5: Airline Requirements**

Airlines may have requirements that are more strict than destination countries. It‚Äôs **your responsibility** to verify and inform us of any additional requirements.

---

**Key Airline Requirements:**

**General:** 
- Confirm airline accepts pets and method (cabin, checked as excess baggage, or cargo) 
- Book well in advance 
- limited pet spaces available 
- Provide health certificate at check-in

**Health Certificate Timing:** 
- Most airlines require a health certificate within 10 days of arrival to destination if pet is traveling as excess baggage or cargo 
- This requirement MAY vary if pet is in-cabin with you 
- Dog and cat requirements may also be different 
- verify with your specific airline

**Temperature Restrictions (for excess baggage or cargo pets):** 
- Airlines typically only fly when temperatures are **45-85¬∞F at BOTH departure and arrival locations** for pet safety 
- Plan travel dates and flight times accordingly to avoid cancellations 
- Check weather forecasts for both departure and arrival cities

**Sedation Policy:** 
- Airlines won‚Äôt fly pets that are medicated and appear sedated in cargo or excess baggage (safety reasons) 
- We carefully evaluate each case for anxiety medication if appropriate

**Specific Crate Requirements:** 
- Airlines have specific crate size, ventilation, and construction requirements 
- Must be airline-approved 
- Verify requirements with your specific airline

**Important:**
Airlines may have requirements more strict than the destination country. Always check with your airline and inform us of any additional needs.

## **Section 6: Common Questions (FAQ)**

### **Q: Can I get CURV after my dog left the US?**

**A:** No.¬†Must be obtained before departure (as of July 31, 2025). Schedule your appointments with us early to ensure this is completed.

### **Q: My dog‚Äôs US rabies vaccination expires while abroad. What happens?**

**A:** Dog becomes foreign-vaccinated ‚Üí [Section 3C](#section-3c) process ($2,000-7,000+). Get 3-year booster before extended travel.

### **Q: Do I need both IHC AND CURV?**

**A:** Possibly. IHC \= enter foreign country. CURV \= return to US from high-risk country. You need IHC if destination requires it. You need CURV if going to high-risk country and returning to US.

### **Q: Can puppies under 6 months enter the US?**

**A:** No exceptions for any dogs from high-risk countries or returning from high-risk countries.

### **Q: Can I drive across from Canada/Mexico?**

**A:** Yes if coming from low-risk country. No if foreign-vaccinated from high-risk country (must fly to facility airport).

### **Q: Do cats need CURV?**

**A:** No.¬†CURV is for dogs only. Cats have minimal CDC import requirements.

### **Q: What about European Union or England travel?**

**A:** EU and England require minimum 21-day wait after primary rabies vaccination. Plan accordingly and schedule early.

### **Q: What about Australia, New Zealand, or Japan?**

**A:** These require 6+ months preparation. Schedule research consultation with us immediately. Do not book travel before consulting with us.

### **Q: What temperatures are safe for pets flying cargo?**

**A:** Airlines typically only fly cargo pets when temperatures are 45-85¬∞F at both departure and arrival cities. Plan travel dates and plane departure/arrival locations accordingly.

### **Q: Do I need to check airline requirements?**

**A:** Yes. Airlines may have requirements stricter than the destination country. It is your responsibility to check and inform us of any additional airline requirements.

### **Q: My departure date is coming up soon and my health certificate hasn‚Äôt been endorsed yet. Should I be worried?**

**A:** We understand your concern\! Once we submit your IHC to USDA for endorsement, the timing is beyond our control. Most endorsements are completed within a few days, but sometimes endorsement occurs just prior to your travel date. We do our best to ensure your pet can travel on time. In rare cases (such as high USDA workload during holiday seasons), endorsement may be delayed, potentially requiring another visit and additional fees. This is why we recommend starting the process 2-6+ months before departure and avoiding peak holiday travel times when possible.

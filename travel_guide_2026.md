## Common Terms & Abbreviations

Understanding these key terms will help you navigate the pet travel process:

### Export vs. Import
- **EXPORT** = Pet leaves the US â†’ Goes to another country
- **IMPORT** = Pet enters the US (includes US pets returning home)
- **Round-trip travel** = Both processes apply

### [IHC (International Health Certificate)](https://www.aphis.usda.gov/pet-travel)
Also called an export health certificate. This is the official document required by most countries to allow your pet to enter. It includes exam findings, vaccination records, and treatments.

### [CURV (Certification of US-Issued Rabies Vaccination)](https://www.cdc.gov/importation/dogs/index.html)
Official CDC document certifying your dog has a valid US rabies vaccination. Required for easy re-entry from [high-risk rabies countries](https://www.cdc.gov/importation/dogs/high-risk-countries.html). **For dogs only.** See Section 3B for details.

### [USDA Endorsement](https://vehcs.aphis.usda.gov/)
Official review and stamp by the US Department of Agriculture on health certificates and CURV forms. Most countries require this. Documents are submitted electronically through VEHCS.

### [VEHCS (Veterinary Export Health Certification System)](https://vehcs.aphis.usda.gov/)
USDA's online system where accredited veterinarians submit and track health certificates and CURV forms for endorsement.

### Green Banner Country
Countries that **accept digital USDA endorsements**. We can email you the endorsed certificate once approved. No physical shipping required.
- **Examples:** Most South American countries
- **Cost advantage:** No shipping fees

### Orange Banner Country
Countries that **require physical USDA endorsement with embossed seal**. We must overnight ship your health certificate once approved.
- **Examples:** All European countries, United Kingdom
- **Additional cost:** $75 overnight shipping fee

### [High-Risk Rabies Country](https://www.cdc.gov/importation/dogs/high-risk-countries.html)
Country on CDC's rabies high-risk list. If your destination is NOT on the list, it's considered low-risk.

### Primary (Initial) Rabies Vaccination
The **first** rabies vaccination given to a dog, OR the first vaccination after any lapse in coverage (even one day when rabies vaccination expired before the next booster). Valid for **only 1 year**, even if the vaccine label says 3 years. Some countries require waiting periods after primary vaccination before travel.

**Solution:** Get a **3-year rabies booster** before extended travel so your CURV stays valid longer.

## Section 4: Common Questions (FAQ)

### Q: Can I get CURV after my dog left the US?
**A:** No. Must be obtained before departure (as of July 31, 2025). Schedule your appointments with us early to ensure this is completed.

### Q: My dog's US rabies vaccination expires while abroad. What happens?
**A:** Dog becomes foreign-vaccinated â†’ Section 3C process ($2,000-7,000+). Get 3-year booster before extended travel.

### Q: Do I need both IHC AND CURV?
**A:** Possibly. IHC = enter foreign country. CURV = return to US from high-risk country. You need IHC if destination requires it. You need CURV if going to high-risk country and returning to US.

### Q: Can puppies under 6 months enter the US?
**A:** No exceptions for any dogs from high-risk countries or returning from high-risk countries.

### Q: Can I drive across from Canada/Mexico?
**A:** Yes if coming from low-risk country. No if foreign-vaccinated from high-risk country (must fly to facility airport).

### Q: Do cats need CURV?
**A:** No. CURV is for dogs only. Cats have minimal CDC import requirements.

### Q: What about European Union or England travel?
**A:** EU and England require minimum 21-day wait after primary rabies vaccination. Plan accordingly and schedule early.

### Q: What about Australia, New Zealand, or Japan?
**A:** These require 6+ months preparation. Schedule research consultation with us immediately. Do not book travel before consulting with us.

### Q: What temperatures are safe for pets flying cargo?
**A:** Airlines typically only fly cargo pets when temperatures are 45-85Â°F at both departure and arrival cities. Plan travel dates and plane departure/arrival locations accordingly.

### Q: Do I need to check airline requirements?
**A:** Yes. Airlines may have requirements stricter than the destination country. It is your responsibility to check and inform us of any additional airline requirements.
